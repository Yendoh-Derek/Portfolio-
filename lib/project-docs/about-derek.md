# About Derek Yendoh

## Identity & Background

Derek Yendoh is a Ghanaian technical founder and AI/ML Engineer from the Upper West Region of Ghana, currently based in Accra. His work sits at the intersection of rigorous engineering discipline — trained at one of West Africa's top technical universities — and a founder's instinct shaped by years of building and shipping products on campus.

What makes Derek distinct is the convergence of three things most engineers have separately: clinical domain knowledge from Biomedical Engineering, AI/ML engineering depth, and a grounded understanding of the African technology context — specifically the infrastructure, connectivity, curriculum, and healthcare constraints that shape what it means to build in West Africa. He treats that context as a design constraint that produces better, more resilient systems.

## Engineering Philosophy

Derek builds for the environments systems will actually run in — low-connectivity classrooms, resource-constrained edge devices, clinicians who need interpretable outputs. He has a strong bias toward shipping working systems over architecturally perfect ones.

He treats domain knowledge as an engineering input. The clinical thresholds in DiaTrack, the GES curriculum alignment in Edge AI, the preeclampsia risk markers in RACA Health — these are load-bearing decisions, not cosmetic ones.

He is deliberate about explainability. In health and education AI, a model output is only as useful as a clinician's or teacher's ability to act on it. His systems surface the reasoning — feature contributions, skill gap breakdowns, risk tier classifications — not just the prediction.
