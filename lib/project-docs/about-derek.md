# About Derek Yendoh

## Identity & Background

Derek Yendoh is a Ghanaian technical founder and builder from the Upper West Region of Ghana, currently based in Accra. He is an AI/ML Engineer and general software engineer with a deep focus on building production-grade AI systems, intelligent APIs, and venture-backed health and education technology products. His work is grounded in a dual identity — rigorous engineering discipline trained at one of West Africa's top technical universities, and a founder's instinct shaped by years of building, shipping, and learning from failure while still on campus.

## Entrepreneurial Work

### Edge AI (Active, Co-founder)

Edge AI is an AI tutoring platform built for Ghanaian and West African secondary school students, aligned to the Ghana Education Service curriculum. It combines a diagnostic assessment engine that identifies per-student literacy and numeracy skill gaps with a live AI tutor that delivers personalized voice-interactive lessons via a streaming pipeline. The platform is built for teachers as much as students — giving classrooms of 40+ students the kind of individualized attention that only one-on-one tutoring could previously provide. Edge AI is Derek's primary active venture, targeting the Ghana market first with a phased expansion into Nigeria, Kenya, and broader Anglophone and Francophone Africa.

### RACA Health (Co-founder)

RACA Health is a wearables-based health monitoring system for pregnant women and high-risk patients of preeclampsia, diabetes, and hypertension. The system uses wearable sensors to continuously monitor patient health vitals and deliver real-time risk predictions, surfacing early warning signals before they become clinical emergencies. RACA Health won the **Africa Health Collaborative grant from the Mastercard Foundation** — a competitive pan-African health innovation grant. It is one of Derek's most significant entrepreneurial achievements and reflects his commitment to applying AI to health challenges specific to the African context.

### Earlier Startup Experience

The ability to ship, learn, and redirect is something he developed on campus, not after.

## Engineering Philosophy

Derek builds things that are honest about their constraints. His projects are designed for the environments they'll actually run in — low-connectivity classrooms in Ghana, resource-constrained edge devices, clinicians who need interpretable outputs, not black boxes. He has a strong bias toward shipping working systems over architecturally perfect ones, and a deep respect for the difference between a demo and a product.

He treats domain knowledge as an engineering input, not a soft add-on. The clinical thresholds in DiaTrack, the GES curriculum alignment in Edge AI, the preeclampsia risk markers in RACA Health — these are not cosmetic. They are load-bearing decisions that make the systems actually useful to the people they serve.

He is also deliberate about explainability. In health and education AI, the output of a model is only as useful as the clinician's or teacher's ability to act on it. Derek's systems are designed to surface the reasoning — feature contributions, skill gap breakdowns, risk tier classifications — not just the prediction.

---

## The Ghanaian Context as a Differentiator

Derek is explicit about his Ghanaian context being a differentiator, not just a biographical detail. Building for Ghana and West Africa means designing for intermittent connectivity, government curriculum frameworks (GES), local languages, local names, local currency, and health systems where diagnostic infrastructure is sparse and wearable-based monitoring can close a real gap. His products use Ghanaian names (Ama, Kwame, Kofi), GH₵ in educational content, Ghanaian historical passages for literacy tasks, and health monitoring frameworks suited to the disease burden of the region.

This context-first design philosophy is what separates his work from generic AI products deployed into African markets as an afterthought. Derek builds from inside the context, not toward it.
